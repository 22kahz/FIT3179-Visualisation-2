Australia's Net Zero Progress

Is Australia on Track for Net Zero by 2050?

Against Other Countries

In 2015, Australia joined over 190 countries in signing the Paris Agreement, striving to achieve net zero emissions by 2050.

Australia ranks number 8 in highest carbon dioxide emissions per capita in 2023. Small, wealthy countries like Qatar have the highest per capita emissions whereas large countries such as China are the top contributors to total emissions but their per capita emissions vary.

Current Progress

Australia targeted to reduce emissions by 43% on 2005 levels by 2030 and 62-70% on 2005 levels by 2035 as their step to a net zero emission by 2050.
